## System Requirements

- A device with a **functioning webcam** (required for hand detection)
- A **well-lit environment** for accurate landmark detection
- A **larger screen** is preferred for the best experience
- A modern browser with WebAssembly and WebGL support (Chrome or Edge recommended)

## Supported Gestures

The app recognizes four distinct gestures, detected from raw hand landmark data without using the `GestureRecognizer` task:

| Gesture | Hand Pose | Description |
|---|---|---|
| **HOVER** 🤚 | Open hand, fingers extended | The base/default state. The tip of the index finger acts as the cursor. Move your hand to navigate. |
| **CLICK** 👌 | Thumb tip and index finger tip pinched together | Triggers a click on the element currently under the cursor. Euclidean distance between landmarks 4 and 8 must be below 0.25. |
| **UNDO** ✋ | All five fingers fully extended, wrist below all fingertips | Undoes the last action. Equivalent to pressing the **Backspace** key. |
| **GRAB** ✊ | All fingers closed into a fist | Opens the information modal overlay. |

<Info>
  The gesture detection logic supports both **left and right hands**. The app reads the `handednesses` result from MediaPipe to determine which hand is in frame and applies the appropriate detection logic for each.
</Info>

## How to Use the Demo

<Steps>
  <Step title="Allow Webcam Access">
    When prompted by the browser, grant camera permission. The app will begin streaming your webcam feed and loading the MediaPipe Hand Landmarker model.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Hold Your Hand Up">
    Position your hand clearly in front of the camera in a well-lit area. The app will display a semi-transparent overlay showing detected landmarks and connectors drawn on your hand.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Use HOVER to Navigate">
    Extend your fingers in an open-hand pose. Your index finger tip acts as the cursor. Move your hand to position the cursor over ATM buttons.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Use CLICK to Interact">
    Pinch your thumb and index finger together (OK gesture) to click the button under the cursor.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Use UNDO to Correct">
    Spread all five fingers fully and hold your hand high (wrist below fingertips) to undo your last input. You can also press the **Backspace** key as an alternative.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Use GRAB for Info">
    Close your hand into a fist to open the information modal.
  </Step>
</Steps>
